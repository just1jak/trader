# A-Liner Replication - Requirements Document

## Project Overview
DIY hard-sided pop-up camper build. Engineering calculators built. Key patents expired.

## Market Research

### Target Market Segments
1. **DIY Enthusiasts** - People who enjoy building their own gear (tiny house, RV, overlanding communities)
2. **Budget-Conscious Campers** - Those wanting hard-sided pop-up benefits without retail markup
3. **Overlanding/Off-Road Community** - Adventurers needing lightweight, capable campers
4. **Workshop/Education** - Makerspaces, vocational schools teaching fabrication skills
5. **Vintage Camper Restorers** - People restoring old A-Liners who need parts/reproduction guidance

### Market Size & Trends
- US RV market: ~$60B industry, growing post-pandemic
- Pop-up campers: ~15% of RV towables market
- DIY RV building niche growing with maker movement
- Increased interest in lightweight, fuel-efficient camping options
- Patent expiration creates opportunity for recreation of classic designs
- Rising costs of new RVs driving interest in used/DIY alternatives

### Competitive Landscape
- **New Manufacturers**:
  - Forest River (Rockwood pop-ups)
  - Jayco (Jay Series pop-ups)
  - Coachmen (Clipper pop-ups)
  - Aliner (original company still exists)
- **Used Market**:
  - eBay, Craigslist, RVTrader for used A-Liners
  - Specialized vintage RV dealers
- **DIY Resources**:
  - YouTube channels (Outdoors RV, etc.)
  - RV forums (iRV2, RV.net)
  - Instructables, Pinterest for build guides
- **Similar Concepts**:
  - Lite-Brite teardrops
  - Aliner-inspired builds by various fabricators

### Differentiation Opportunities
- Exact replication of expired-patent A-Liner designs
- Modern materials/lighter weight versions
- Comprehensive build guides with engineering calculations
- Customization options (solar, off-grid, interior layouts)
- Community of builders sharing modifications
- Potential for CNC-cut kits or partial kit sales

## Revenue Models

### 1. Plans/Kits Sales
- **Digital Plans** ($29-49): Detailed CAD drawings, BOM, build guide
- **Partial Kits** ($199-499): Pre-cut frame pieces, specialized hardware
- **Full Kits** ($2,999-4,999): Everything needed except lumber/fabric
- **Engineering Consultation** ($99/hour): Custom modifications, structural analysis

### 2. Education/Workshops
- Online course ($79-149): Video build-along series
- In-person workshops ($299-499/weekend): Guided build events
- Certification program for builders/installers

### 3. Affiliate/Advertising
- Referral fees for recommended components (axles, lifts, fabrics)
- Sponsorship from camping/outdoor brands
- Advertising in builder community newsletter

### 4. Custom Builds
- Commissioned builds for clients ($5,000-8,000)
- Prototype development for specific use cases
- Restoration services for vintage A-Liners

### 5. Licensing
- License plans to other builders/fabricators
- White-label kits for RV dealers to offer DIY option

## Technical Requirements

### Core Features
- Structural engineering calculations for lift mechanism
- Material specifications (wood types, thicknesses, fasteners)
- Detailed assembly instructions with photos/diagrams
- Parts sourcing guide (where to buy specialized components)
- Electrical system options (12V, solar integration)
- Plumbing options (fresh water, grey/black water tanks)
- Interior layout variations (bed, dinette, storage)
- Weatherproofing and sealing details
- Weight distribution and towing calculations
- Safety considerations (load ratings, emergency egress)

### Documentation Needs
- CAD drawings (SketchUp, Fusion 360, or 2D PDFs)
- Bill of Materials with sources and costs
- Step-by-step build guide (PDF/video)
- Troubleshooting FAQ
- Maintenance schedule
- Modification gallery (inspiration for customizations)
- Legal/safety disclaimers

### Tech Stack for Delivery
- Website: Static site generator (Jekyll, Hugo) or simple WordPress
- File Hosting: PDF downloads, video streaming (YouTube unlisted or Vimeo)
- Community: Forum (Discourse) or Discord server
- Email: Newsletter service (ConvertKit, MailerLite)
- Payments: Gumroad, SendOwl, or Shopify Lite
- Analytics: Google Analytics or Plausible

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Content Building (Month 1-2)
- Document existing engineering calculators
- Create detailed build guide for one standard model
- Build prototype to validate instructions
- Create YouTube teaser content showing process
- Engage with vintage A-Liner owner groups

### Phase 2: Launch (Month 3)
- Sell digital plans on Gumroad/Etsy
- Launch YouTube build-along series
- Post in DIY/RV forums (iRV2, Reddit r/DIY, r/Camping)
- Reach out to RV bloggers for reviews
- Offer early-bird discount for first 50 buyers

### Phase 3: Expansion (Month 4-6)
- Develop multiple model options (different sizes/layouts)
- Create partial kit offerings
- Build email list through free mini-guide lead magnet
- Partner with component suppliers for affiliate deals
- Consider creating a builder certification program

### Phase 4: Scaling (Month 6-12)
- Explore CNC cutting services for kit production
- Develop off-grid/solar specialty packages
- Create restoration guide for vintage models
- Attend RV shows (virtual or in-person) for exposure
- Consider Amazon/Kindle distribution for printed guide
