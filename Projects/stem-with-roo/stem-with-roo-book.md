## Page 2
Hey there! I'm Roo. I love figuring out how things work.
Today we're going to learn about electricity!

## Page 3
Electricity is like invisible energy that can make lights shine, motors spin, and computers think.
We use batteries to store electricity safely.

## Page 4
To make electricity do something, we need a path called a circuit.
A circuit is a loop that lets electricity flow.

## Page 5
Let's build a simple circuit!
We need: a battery, a wire, and a light bulb.

## Page 6
First, connect one end of the wire to the top of the battery.
Then connect the other end of the wire to the metal side of the bulb.

## Page 7
Now touch the bottom of the battery to the bottom of the bulb.
If everything is connected, the bulb will light up! 💡

## Page 8
What if we want to turn the light off and on?
We can add a switch!

## Page 9
A switch is like a bridge that can be open or closed.
When the bridge is closed (on), electricity flows and the light shines.
When the bridge is open (off), electricity stops and the light goes dark.

## Page 10
Let's add the switch into our circuit.
Break the wire and attach each end to the switch terminals.

## Page 11
Now flip the switch up to turn the light on, and down to turn it off.
You just made a working light switch!

## Page 12
Fun fact: You can make a switch from a paper clip!
Just bend it so it can touch two contacts.

## Page 13
Try it: Tape one end of the wire to a battery, the other to a bulb,
and use the paper clip to connect them. Flip it to turn the light on and off!

## Page 14
Sometimes the light doesn't turn on.
Let's troubleshoot! Check:
- Is the battery fresh?
- Are all the connections tight?
- Is the wire broken?

## Page 15
If the bulb is loose, tighten it.
If the battery is dead, replace it with a new one.
Almost always, you can fix it!

## Page 16
Important: Always ask an adult before trying experiments.
Only use batteries (like AA or AAA) – never wall outlets.
Stay safe and have fun!
