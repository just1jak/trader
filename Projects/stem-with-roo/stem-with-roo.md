# STEM with Roo - Requirements Document

## Project Overview
Children's STEM education content brand.

## Market Research

### Target Market Segments
1. **Parents of Elementary/Middle School Children** (Ages 5-13) - Primary consumers
2. **Homeschooling Families** - Significant market for structured STEM curriculum
3. **Teachers/Educators** - Looking for supplemental classroom materials
4. **After-School Program Providers** - Needing engaging STEM activities
5. **Gift Givers** - Grandparents, relatives purchasing educational presents
6. **Children Themselves** - Engaging content that kids actually want to do

### Market Size & Trends
- Global STEM education market projected to reach $254.37B by 2029
- US K-12 STEM spending: ~$3B annually
- Homeschooling growth: ~3.7M students in US (2020-2021), increasing
- Parental concern about screen time vs. educational value
- Rise of "edutainment" - content that educates while entertaining
- Increased focus on diversity in STEM (girls, underrepresented minorities)
- Growth in subscription box market for educational content
- Popularity of YouTube educational channels for kids (Crash Course Kids, SciShow Kids)
- Maker movement influence on hands-on STEM learning

### Competitive Landscape
- **Subscription Boxes**:
  - KiwiCo (leading brand with multiple age lines)
  - Mel Science (chemistry-focused)
  - Spangler Science Club
  - Grove Collaborative's STEM boxes
  - Little Passports (geography/culture with STEM elements)
- **Digital Content**:
  - YouTube channels (SciShow Kids, Crash Course Kids, Nat Geo Kids)
  - Educational apps (Khan Academy Kids, Duolingo ABC, Prodigy)
  - Netflix educational series (Emily's Wonder Lab, Brainchild)
  - PBS LearningMedia, Discovery Education
- **Physical Products**:
  - LEGO Education, Snap Circuits
  - Thames & Kosmos science kits
  - National Geographic STEM kits
  - Learning Resources, Educational Insights
- **Curriculum Providers**:
  - Mystery Science (video-based lessons)
  - Generation Genius
  - Science Buddies (free projects)
  - NASA's STEM engagement programs

### Differentiation Opportunities
- Character-driven content (Roo as a relatable STEM guide)
- Focus on everyday science (backyard experiments, kitchen chemistry)
- Integration with outdoor/nature-based learning
- Multi-format approach (videos, printable activities, hands-on projects)
- Emphasis on scientific method and inquiry over just facts
- Parent resources for co-learning and extension activities
- Potential for community/sharing aspects (kid-submitted experiments)
- Alignment with NGSS (Next Generation Science Standards)
- Bilingual or multilingual options for broader reach
- Focus on under-served topics (data science for kids, environmental STEM)

## Revenue Models

### 1. Subscription Model
- **Monthly** ($9.99-14.99/month): New content weekly, access to library
- **Annual** ($99-149/month): Discounted rate, bonus content
- **Tiered Access**:
  - Basic: Digital content only (videos, printables)
  - Standard: Digital + monthly printable activity pack
  - Premium: Digital + quarterly physical kit shipment

### 2. Individual Product Sales
- **Digital Downloads** ($4.99-9.99): Individual experiment guides, lesson packs
- **Video Series** ($14.99-24.99): Themed collections (space, dinosaurs, robots)
- **Physical Kits** ($19.99-39.99): Complete experiment sets with supplies
- **Books** ($12.99-18.99): Illustrated experiment books, activity books

### 3. Advertising/Sponsorship
- YouTube ad revenue (once reaching sufficient viewership)
- Brand sponsorships for specific experiments (science supply companies)
- Affiliate marketing for recommended tools/kits
- Sponsored content from aligned brands (museums, science centers)

### 4. Education/B2B
- School/site licenses for classroom use
- After-school program curriculum packages
- Library/educational institution subscriptions
- Teacher training/workshops (virtual or in-person)

### 5. Live Events/Experiences
- Virtual STEM camps/workshops
- Partnership with museums/science centers for events
- Birthday party packages (virtual or in-person)
- Conference presentations/appearances

## Technical Requirements

### Core Content Types
- **Video Content**:
  - Short-form (3-8 min) experiment demonstrations
  - Longer-form (15-20 min) exploratory episodes
  - Live-streamed or pre-recorded
  - Multiple formats: talking head, demonstration, animation, outdoor
- **Printable Activities**:
  - Worksheets, observation sheets, data tables
  - Coloring pages with educational elements
  - Instruction cards for experiments
  - Achievement/badges certificates
  - Parent guides with extension questions
- **Hands-on Projects**:
  - Step-by-step guides with photos/diagrams
  - Material lists with sourcing alternatives
  - Safety guidelines and supervision notes
  - Troubleshooting tips
  - Variations and extensions
- **Interactive/Digital**:
  - Simple simulations or games (basic HTML5/JavaScript)
  - Interactive quizzes or knowledge checks
  - Progress tracking systems
  - Augmented reality elements (future consideration)

### Tech Stack for Content Delivery
- **Website/Platform**:
  - CMS: WordPress, Ghost, or custom solution
  - Video hosting: YouTube (primary) + Vimeo/Wistia (for private content)
  - File delivery: PDF downloads, possibly protected content
  - Membership system: Memberful, Patreon, or WooCommerce subscriptions
  - Email marketing: ConvertKit, MailerLite for newsletters
  - Payments: Stripe/PayPal integration
- **Production Equipment**:
  - Camera: Smartphone (iPhone) or dedicated mirrorless
  - Audio: Lavalier or shotgun mic for clear sound
  - Lighting: Softbox or ring light for consistent quality
  - Editing software: Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, or DaVinci Resolve
  - Graphics/animation: Canva, Adobe After Effects, or Blender
- **Community/Engagement**:
  - Comments: YouTube comments or website commenting system
  - Social media: Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest for reach
  - Newsletter: Regular updates with free content samples
  - User-generated content: Safe platform for kids to share experiments (with moderation)
  - Live streaming: YouTube Live or Zoom for workshops

### Content Production Workflow
- Idea generation and curriculum alignment
- Scriptwriting and storyboarding
- Filming (experiments, explanations)
- Editing and post-production
- Creation of accompanying printables
- Quality review and safety check
- Publishing and promotion
- Engagement monitoring and feedback incorporation

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Content Building & Audience (Month 1-2)
- Create pilot content (5-10 videos + printables)
- Establish branding and visual identity (Roo character)
- Build simple website with free sample content
- Start YouTube channel with consistent upload schedule
- Engage with parenting/homeschooling communities (Facebook groups, Reddit)
- Offer free samples in exchange for email addresses
- Seek feedback from target audience (parents, kids, educators)

### Phase 2: Launch (Month 3)
- Launch paid subscription with founding member pricing
- YouTube growth strategy (SEO, collaborations, cross-promotion)
- Pinterest optimization for printable activities
- Instagram content showing experiments in action
- Outreach to mommy bloggers, homeschool reviewers
- Consider Amazon Prime Video direct or educational licensing
- Develop launch discount for first 100 subscribers

### Phase 3: Growth (Month 4-6)
- Develop multiple content series (different STEM themes)
- Create seasonal content (summer outdoors, winter indoors)
- Develop physical prototype kits for testing
- Partnership with educational influencers
- Attend (virtual) homeschool conventions or STEM fairs
- Create educator-specific resources (lesson plans, standards alignment)
- Develop referral program (free month for referring friends)

### Phase 4: Expansion (Month 6-12)
- Launch physical subscription kit option
- Create content in additional languages (Spanish first)
- Develop school/classroom licensing model
- Explore television/streaming platform partnerships
- Create live virtual events/camps
- Consider merchandise (Roo plush, branded lab coats)
- Develop assessment/progress tracking features
