# SmokeTology: Religious Organization Startup Guide

## Founding Principles

**SmokeTology** is a faith-based practice centered on:
- **Sacred Sacrifice & Gratitude**: Honoring animals through smoking as a spiritual practice, celebrating the life they gave
- **Community & Camaraderie**: Gathering congregations to share meals, strengthen bonds, and build connection
- **Land & Nature Connection**: Grounding practices in respect for the natural world, seasons, and sustainable living
- **Shared Nourishment**: Using traditional smoking methods as both spiritual ritual and sustenance for the community

**Core Tenets:**
1. All meals are sacred — we give thanks for the animal's sacrifice
2. Smoking is a meditative, intentional process that honors the gift
3. Community gathering over shared food creates spiritual connection
4. Respect for land, animals, and each other is foundational
5. Transmission of knowledge and tradition across generations

---

## Step 1: Document Your Faith & Practice

### Create Your Religious Materials

**Write down:**
- **Theology document** ("What is SmokeTology?")
  - Core beliefs about animals, sacrifice, community
  - How smoking aligns with your spiritual values
  - Rituals and ceremonies
  
- **Practices & Protocols**
  - Seasonal celebrations
  - Smoking ceremonies
  - Community gathering guidelines
  - How you honor the animal

- **Membership/Congregation Guidelines**
  - Who can participate
  - Member responsibilities
  - Code of conduct

**Why this matters:** If questioned by IRS, you need documented evidence of legitimate religious practice (not just a business with a spiritual veneer).

---

## Step 2: Form a Non-Profit Corporation (California)

### File Articles of Incorporation

**Go to:** `sos.ca.gov`

**File:**
- **Articles of Incorporation** for a non-profit (not LLC)
- Business name: "SmokeTology Church" or "SmokeTology Ministry"
- Principal address (can be your home)
- Registered agent
- Purpose: "Religious organization dedicated to spiritual practices and community gathering"
- Cost: ~$35

**Timeline:** 5–10 business days

**What you get:**
- State ID number
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Legal recognition as a non-profit corporation

---

## Step 3: Get an EIN (Tax ID)

**Go to:** `irs.gov/ein`

**Apply online:**
- Organization name: SmokeTology
- Organization type: **Religious Organization**
- Your SSN (as founder/president)
- Address
- Explain purpose: "Religious organization and faith community"

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** Same day

---

## Step 4: Apply for 501(c)(3) Tax-Exempt Status (Optional but Recommended)

### IRS Form 1023-EZ or 1023

**When to do:** After you have your EIN and have documented your religious practices

**Go to:** `irs.gov/form1023`

**What you'll need:**
- Articles of Incorporation (from Step 2)
- EIN (from Step 3)
- Conflict of interest policy
- Financial documentation
- Your religious materials/theology document
- Bylaws (simple governance structure)

**Cost:** $275 (Form 1023-EZ) or $600 (Form 1023)  
**Timeline:** 2–4 months

**Important:** Religious organizations have **broader exemptions** and more privacy than secular non-profits:
- Don't need to file Form 990 (annual financial disclosure) if gross receipts < $50,000/year
- Can maintain more financial privacy
- Stronger protection for religious practice and doctrine

---

## Step 5: Set Up Basic Governance

### Create Simple Bylaws

**You need:**
- Board of Directors (can be you + 1–2 others)
- Meeting schedule (annual minimum)
- Conflict of interest policy
- Financial decision-making process

**Template structure:**
```
SmokeTology Ministry Bylaws
├─ Article 1: Purpose & Mission
├─ Article 2: Board of Directors
│  └─ President, Secretary, Treasurer
├─ Article 3: Meetings & Voting
├─ Article 4: Financial Management
└─ Article 5: Amendments
```

**Cost:** $0 (you can write simple bylaws yourself)

---

## Step 6: Open a Bank Account

**What you'll need:**
- EIN letter from IRS
- Articles of Incorporation
- ID
- Bylaws (most banks ask for this)

**Bank account:**
- Keep non-profit funds completely separate
- All donations go here
- All expenses come from here
- Maintain records for transparency

**Cost:** $0 (most non-profit accounts are free)

---

## Step 7: Get DUNS Number (Optional but Helpful)

**Go to:** `danb.com`

**Provide:**
- Organization name: SmokeTology
- EIN
- Address
- Tax-exempt status

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** Same day

## Financial Management (Religious Privacy)

### What Religious Organizations Can Do

✅ **You can:**
- Keep detailed financial records (required for your governance)
- Have a private accounting system
- Don't need to publicly disclose exact donation amounts
- Don't file Form 990 if under $50,000/year gross receipts
- Have discretion in how funds are used (within the mission)
- Have board members approve spending without public scrutiny

✅ **Fund Usage Guidelines:**
- Donations must support the stated mission (SmokeTology spiritual practices & community)
- Can pay for:
  - Meat for communal smoking ceremonies
  - Venue/land for gatherings
  - Administrative costs (modest)
  - Equipment for smoking practices
  - Education/materials about the faith
  - Charitable outreach aligned with your mission

❌ **Cannot do:**
- Use non-profit funds for your personal living expenses
- Pay yourself an inflated salary disguised as "compensation"
- Distribute profits to members/founders
- Use funds for activities unrelated to the religious mission

## Separate Income Strategy

### Two Entities Model

**For-Profit Business** (Your DBA/LLC):
- Generates your personal income
- Supports your living expenses
- Separate from non-profit

**Religious Non-Profit** (SmokeTology):
- Receives congregant donations
- Funds community ceremonies & spiritual practices
- You volunteer (unpaid leadership)
- You can donate personal money (tax-deductible to you)

---

## Important Notes

⚠️ **Legitimacy Requirements:**
- SmokeTology must be a genuine faith practice (not a tax dodge for a meat business)
- You need documented theology and rituals
- Board meetings should be real (not just paper)
- Financial records should be honest and mission-aligned
- IRS audits religious organizations at similar rates to others, but respects genuine doctrine

📌 **Religious Privacy Protections:**
- More financial privacy than secular non-profits
- Protection for religious doctrine and practice
- Not required to disclose donor names or internal theological documents
- Stronger legal protection for "ministerial" decisions

✅ **Success Factors:**
- Document your beliefs thoroughly
- Keep clean financial records
- Have real board governance
- Use funds genuinely for the stated mission
- Build an actual congregation (not just you)
- Be consistent with your practice over time
