# Permit Roadmap: APN 086-631-14 (CUP + Glamping) & APN 086-521-02 (Primary Residence)

**Last Updated:** 2026-05-16  
**Project:** Glamping STR / Land Acquisition  
**Parcels:** SU (9.95ac glamping) + RA (1.49ac residence)

---

## Overview

This document provides sequential, actionable steps to obtain:
1. **APN 086-631-14 (Special Use zoning):** Conditional Use Permit (CUP) for 5+ site glamping operation
2. **APN 086-521-02 (RA zoning):** Building Permit for new primary residence + utilities

**Critical Path:** Both can run in parallel. CUP (14–25 weeks) is the long pole; primary residence (8–12 weeks) completes before glamping is operational.

**Total Timeline:** 4–7 months from application to both permits in hand.

---

# PART 1: APN 086-631-14 (SU Zoning) — Conditional Use Permit for Glamping

## Phase 0: Pre-Entitlement Research & Pre-Application (Weeks 1–2)

### Step 1a: Schedule Pre-Application Meeting with County Planning
**Action:** Call Santa Cruz County Planning Department at **831-454-2702**

**Request in call:**
- "Schedule a 30–60 minute pre-application meeting with the Zoning Administrator to discuss a glamping/campground operation on 9.95 acres (APN 086-631-14, SU zoning)."
- Ask for: CUP application fee (2026 current rate), processing timeline, Hearing Examiner docket backlog
- Ask about: Definition of "Permanent Structure" in glamping context — tents vs. platforms vs. cabins
- Ask about: Can you phase the permit (primitive → glamping) or must CUP cover final state?

**Cost:** Free to $200  
**Timeline:** 1 week scheduling + meeting

**Why it matters:** Direct guidance from staff often reveals policy preferences not in published code, and you'll get accurate fee/timeline estimates.

---

### Step 1b: Obtain Parcel Coordinates & Coastal Commission Jurisdiction Verification
**Action:** 
1. Visit Santa Cruz County Assessor GIS: assessor.sccgov.org (public property search)
2. Enter APN: 086-631-14
3. Note exact coordinates (latitude/longitude)
4. Screenshot/save parcel boundary map

**Cross-reference Coastal Commission jurisdiction:**
- Visit California Coastal Commission LCP viewer: coastal.ca.gov/lcp
- Search SCC county parcels
- Confirm: **Parcel should be 8+ miles inland, outside Coastal Zone = NO CC jurisdiction needed**
- If result shows "Coastal Zone boundary intersects parcel" → escalate to Planning Dept

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** 1 day (online research)

**Expected result:** Parcel is outside Coastal Commission jurisdiction; CEQA review only.

---

### Step 1c: Request County CEQA Checklist & Environmental Review Template
**Action:** Same call (831-454-2702), or email county.planning@sccgov.org

**Request:**
- "Provide CEQA Initial Study checklist for a commercial campground project (discretionary project, lead agency is County Planning)"
- "What are typical mitigation measures for glamping on SCC mountain land?" (biological survey, traffic, visual, noise, stormwater)
- "Is the project likely to require an EIR, or are mitigations sufficient for a Negative Declaration?"

**Cost:** $0 (public record)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

**Expected result:** Draft mitigation measures ready before formal application.

---

### Step 1d: Research Zoning Code — SU District Allowed/Conditional Uses
**Action:** Obtain Santa Cruz County Code Chapter 13.20 (SU District)

**Access:**
- codepublishing.com/CA/SantaCruzCounty/html/ (automated blockers; try manual PDF download)
- library.municode.com/ca/santa_cruz_county (JS-rendered; contact County if links fail)
- **Or call County Planning (831-454-2702):** "Mail/email me a copy of SCC Chapter 13.20 (Special Use District zoning code)"

**Key sections to extract:**
- Allowed uses (without permit)
- Conditional uses (require CUP per 13.28)
- Dimensional standards (setbacks, height, lot coverage)
- Glamping-specific language (if any)

**Cost:** $0–20 (printing/shipping if requested by mail)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

---

## Phase 1: Environmental Review (CEQA) — (Weeks 2–12)

### Step 2a: Prepare Initial Study (IS) Outline
**Action:** Using the CEQA checklist from Step 1c, draft an Initial Study outline covering:

1. **Project Description**
   - 9.95-acre parcel, SU zoning, unimproved land
   - Proposed glamping operation: 5–12 campsites (determine final #)
   - Phase ladder: primitive → enhanced → low-impact utilities → full glamping
   - Years 1–3 buildout timeline

2. **Environmental Impacts Analysis**
   - **Land Use:** Zoning consistency (SU allows conditional use)
   - **Traffic:** Nightly guest arrivals; peak season estimate
   - **Utilities:** Septic system, water supply (well vs. trucked?), electrical (on-grid vs. solar)
   - **Biological Resources:** Habitat survey (forested mountain land → likely forest habitat; any endangered species?)
   - **Visual Impact:** Mountain views; setback requirements for structures
   - **Noise:** Guest activity, quiet hours enforcement
   - **Hydrology:** Stormwater, erosion control (hillside site)

3. **Proposed Mitigations**
   - Biological survey (Phase 1, before ground disturbance)
   - Traffic/parking plan
   - Stormwater management (detention, erosion control)
   - Noise ordinance compliance (quiet hours 10 PM–7 AM)
   - Visual screening (trees, berms)
   - Septic system design (on-site approval)

**Cost:** $0 (internal)  
**Timeline:** 1 week (outline), 2–3 weeks (detailed draft)

---

### Step 2b: Commission Biological Survey (If Needed)
**Action:** Contact a qualified biologist familiar with Santa Cruz Mountains habitat

**Scope:**
- Phase 1 ESA (Environmental Site Assessment) — document existing vegetation, wildlife, water features
- Focused species survey (if County checklist flags endangered species risk)
- Mitigations (habitat preservation, construction timing, etc.)

**Cost:** $1,500–4,000 (Phase 1 ESA); $3,000–8,000 (if focused survey needed)  
**Timeline:** 4–8 weeks (can run parallel with CUP app preparation)

**When to order:** Immediately after pre-application meeting if County Planning suggests survey is likely required.

---

### Step 2c: File Notice of Preparation (NOP) with County Planning
**Action:** Submit Initial Study + NOP to SCC Planning Department (address provided in pre-app meeting)

**Contents:**
- Cover letter: Project description, proposed uses, environmental review scope
- Draft IS (from Step 2b)
- Parcel map + proposed site plan (conceptual is fine at this stage)
- Proponent contact info

**Cost:** $100–300 (NOP filing fee; exact amount from pre-app call)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks intake + 30-day public comment period

---

### Step 2d: Monitor NOP Public Comment Period (30 days)
**Action:** County mails/publishes NOP; 30-day comment period begins

**What to do:**
- Note deadline for public comments
- Prepare responses to any concerns raised (e.g., traffic, noise, habitat)
- If significant new impacts emerge from comments → prepare Negative Declaration addendum

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** 30 days (concurrent with CUP application prep)

---

### Step 2e: Finalize Negative Declaration (ND)
**Action:** Incorporate public comments into draft ND; submit to County Planning

**Contents:**
- Executive summary (project doesn't cause significant unavoidable impacts)
- Findings table (each checklist item: No impact / Less than significant / Significant but mitigated / Significant unavoidable)
- Mitigation measures (from IS)
- Statement of overriding considerations (if any significant but mitigated impacts)

**Cost:** $0 (internal)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks (assuming no EIR alternative needed)

---

### Step 2f: County Certifies ND (CEQA Clearance)
**Action:** County Planning reviews ND, posts for public review if required, certifies completion

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks  
**Expected result:** CEQA clearance letter = green light for CUP hearing

---

## Phase 2: Conditional Use Permit (CUP) Application (Weeks 12–25)

### Step 3a: Prepare Full CUP Application Package
**Action:** Assemble comprehensive application materials per SCC Chapter 13.28 (CUP application requirements)

**Application contents (typical for glamping on SU):**

1. **Cover Letter**
   - Project title, parcel ID (086-631-14), applicant info
   - Statement: "CUP requested for commercial glamping operation on 9.95 acres, SU zoning per SCC 13.28"
   - Estimated timeline + staff review request

2. **Applicant Qualifications**
   - Ownership proof (deed or title report)
   - Experience: Prior hospitality/campground operations (if any)
   - References / letters of support (neighbors, local officials)

3. **Site Plan (Scaled, 1" = 100' or similar)**
   - Parcel boundary + adjoining properties
   - Proposed campsite locations (5–12 sites)
   - Access road layout
   - Utility infrastructure (septic, water, electrical)
   - Parking area, check-in building (if any)
   - Buffer zones / setbacks from property line
   - Vegetation / screening

4. **Operational Plan**
   - Number of campsites + occupancy limits
   - Season of operation (year-round? summer only?)
   - Guest arrival/departure patterns (peak vs. off-season)
   - Staffing model (owner-operated? live-in manager?)
   - Trash/recycling management
   - Noise mitigation (quiet hours, sound baffles)
   - Fire safety plan + evacuation routes
   - Parking capacity + guest transport

5. **Land Use Consistency Narrative**
   - Explain: How does glamping align with SU zoning district character?
   - Address: Public benefits (employment, tax revenue, tourism)
   - Explain: Why glamping use is compatible with neighboring properties

6. **Environmental Assessment Results**
   - Certified Negative Declaration (from CEQA, Phase 1 Step 2e)
   - Mitigation Monitoring Plan

7. **Zoning Compliance Checklist**
   - Per SCC 13.28: Address each approval criterion
   - Examples: Land use compatibility, no traffic hazards, public welfare protected, etc.

**Cost:** $0–500 (legal review optional)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks to assemble

---

### Step 3b: Submit CUP Application to County Planning
**Action:** File application package with cover letter at SCC Planning Department

**Required:**
- Original + 4–6 copies (check with Planning Dept in pre-app meeting)
- Application fee: $2,000–4,000 (from pre-app call)
- Cover letter requesting completeness review

**What to expect:**
- Planning Dept reviews application for completeness (2–3 weeks)
- You may receive a list of missing items → revise + resubmit
- Once "complete," Hearing Examiner docket is scheduled

**Cost:** $2,000–4,000 (application fee)  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks completeness review

---

### Step 3c: Attend Planning Department Pre-Hearing Conference (If Offered)
**Action:** Some counties require a pre-hearing conference to narrow issues and streamline the hearing

**What to prepare:**
- Slide deck or visuals (site photos, site plan, project renderings)
- One-page project summary
- Responses to likely Planning Dept questions (traffic, noise, compatibility)

**Cost:** $0 (usually)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks before hearing

---

### Step 3d: Hearing Examiner Hearing & Decision
**Action:** Attend hearing before Hearing Examiner to present project + respond to public comments

**Typical hearing structure:**
- Applicant presentation (10–15 min): Project overview, benefits, mitigations
- County Planning Dept staff report + recommendation
- Public comment period (neighbors, interested parties)
- Applicant rebuttal
- Hearing Examiner takes case under advisement

**Timeline to decision:** 1–4 weeks post-hearing (examiner issues written decision)

**Hearing Examiner appearance fee:** $500–1,500 (from pre-app call)

**Possible outcomes:**
- ✅ **Approved:** CUP issued
- ✅ **Approved with conditions:** CUP issued; you must comply with conditions (septic design review, biological surveys, annual reporting, etc.)
- ❌ **Denied:** Need to appeal or revise proposal

**Cost:** $500–1,500 (examiner fee)  
**Timeline:** 4–8 weeks from completeness to hearing + decision

---

### Step 3e: CUP Decision & Appeal Period
**Action:** County issues written CUP decision

**If approved:**
- 10-day appeal period (neighbors/public can appeal to County Board of Supervisors)
- After 10 days with no appeal → CUP is final & enforceable

**If appeal filed:**
- Board of Supervisors hears appeal (2–8 weeks)
- Decision upholds, reverses, or modifies Examiner decision

**Cost:** $0 (unless you need to respond to appeal)  
**Timeline:** 10 days + potential 2–8 weeks if appealed

---

## Phase 3: Post-CUP Permits (Weeks 25–28)

### Step 4a: Obtain Zoning Compliance Certificate
**Action:** After CUP approval, submit zoning compliance request to County Planning

**Contents:**
- "Request zoning compliance certificate for glamping operation, CUP [case number]"
- Shows: Project complies with zoning + CUP approval

**Cost:** $0–50  
**Timeline:** 1 week

**Why:** Required for subsequent building permits + STR registration.

---

### Step 4b: File Organized Camp Permit Application (If 5+ Campsites)
**Action:** Simultaneous with post-CUP work, apply for Organized Camp Permit (SCC Chapter 7.44)

**Contents:**
- Property ownership proof
- Site plan (same as CUP)
- Operational plan (capacity, season, safety)
- Fire safety plan + evacuation routes
- Sanitation plan (composting toilets? septic treatment levels?)
- Insurance certificate ($1M general liability)

**Multi-department review:**
- Fire Department: Life safety, egress, water access, equipment
- Health Department: Sanitation, utilities, food service (if applicable)

**Cost:** $300–600 (permit fee) + $150–300 per inspection (2–3 inspections)  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks Fire review + 2–4 weeks Health review (concurrent)

---

### Step 4c: Obtain Fire Department Approval
**Action:** Fire Marshal inspection + approval for glamping site

**Inspection covers:**
- Egress routes (minimum 2 exits from each site)
- Water supply for firefighting (hydrants, ponds, tanks)
- Access roads (width, grade, weight limits)
- Fuel management / defensible space (vegetation clearance)
- Emergency signage + lighting

**Cost:** Included in camp permit fee  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks (can overlap with Health Dept)

**Expected result:** Fire sign-off + any required mitigations (e.g., water tank installation, road widening).

---

### Step 4d: Obtain Health Department Approval
**Action:** Health Dept sanitation + utilities inspection

**Covers:**
- Septic system (design review, drainfield sizing)
- Water system (well vs. potable trucked water; testing requirements)
- Food service (if on-site restaurant; otherwise N/A)
- Garbage/recycling management
- Greywater disposal

**Cost:** Included in camp permit fee  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks (concurrent with Fire)

**Expected result:** Health sign-off + any required system designs (septic design engineer engagement may be needed).

---

### Step 4e: Organize Camp Permit Issuance
**Action:** Both Fire + Health approve → SCC Planning issues Organized Camp Permit

**Contents:**
- Permit number + effective date
- Conditions of operation (capacity limits, season, required inspections)
- Annual renewal requirement ($200–400/yr)

**Cost:** $200–400/yr renewal (included in initial permit fee)  
**Timeline:** 1 week (after Fire + Health approvals)

---

## Phase 4: Building Permits for Structures (Weeks 28–35)

### Step 5a: Develop Detailed Structure Plans
**Action:** For any permanent/semi-permanent structures (platforms, cabins, check-in building), prepare architect/engineer drawings

**Required by Building Dept:**
- Foundation design (if permanent)
- Structural plans (if structure > temporary)
- Utility connections (electrical, water, septic)
- Fire egress (doors, windows)
- Accessibility (if public-facing, ADA compliance)

**For glamping tents/canvas structures:**
- If temporary (<180 days/year): May be exempt from building permit
- If permanent: Treated as structure; full building permit required

**Cost:** $2,000–8,000 (engineering/architecture)  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks

---

### Step 5b: Submit Building Permit Applications
**Action:** File with SCC Building & Safety Division

**Application package (per site/structure):**
- Completed permit form
- Site plan + structure location
- Architectural/engineering drawings
- Septic design (if structure tied to septic)
- Electrical one-line diagram (if permanent power)

**Cost:** Building permit fee = $200–1,000 per structure (schedule-based)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks intake + 2–4 weeks plan review

---

### Step 5c: Building Department Plan Review & Conditional Approval
**Action:** County Building Dept reviews plans for code compliance

**Typical review scope:**
- Structural engineering (IRC)
- Fire/life safety (doors, egress, sprinklers if required)
- Electrical (NEC code)
- Plumbing (IPC code)
- Accessibility (IBC accessibility chapter)

**Likely outcome:** "Approved with minor revisions" or "Approved as submitted" (major revisions = resubmit).

**Cost:** $0 (included in permit fee)  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks

---

### Step 5d: On-Site Construction & Inspections
**Action:** Build structures per approved plans; request building inspections at key phases

**Inspection milestones:**
- Foundation inspection (before structure built)
- Framing inspection (before interior finish)
- Electrical/plumbing rough-in inspection
- Final inspection (upon completion)

**Cost:** $0 (included in permit fee; ~$100–300 per inspection if extra inspections needed)  
**Timeline:** Concurrent with construction (typically 4–12 weeks depending on structure type)

---

### Step 5e: Building Permit Final Sign-Off
**Action:** After final inspection passed, Building Dept issues Certificate of Occupancy (CO) or Permit Closure

**CO means:** Structure is legal to occupy/use.

**Cost:** $0 (included in permit fee)  
**Timeline:** 1 week

---

## Phase 5: STR Registration & Revenue Launch (Weeks 28–35)

### Step 6a: Obtain Liability Insurance
**Action:** Contact insurance broker with hospitality/glamping experience

**Request quotes for:**
- Premises liability: $1M minimum for glamping operations
- Coverage: Guest injury, property damage, alcohol liability (if applicable)
- Annual cost estimate: $1,000–2,000

**Cost:** $1,000–2,000/yr  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

**Required for:** STR registration + Camp Permit.

---

### Step 6b: File STR Registration Application
**Action:** Submit to SCC Assessor–Clerk Recorder or Planning Dept (confirm in pre-app)

**Application contents:**
- Property ownership proof (deed)
- Zoning compliance certificate (from Step 4a)
- Insurance certificate copy
- Operational plan (occupancy limits, nightly rate estimate, season)
- Owner contact info + emergency contact

**Cost:** $150–300 (annual registration fee)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks intake + processing

---

### Step 6c: STR Registration Approved & TOT Account Setup
**Action:** SCC approves registration; setup Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) account with Assessor–Clerk Recorder

**Monthly obligation:**
- Collect 10–12% TOT from each nightly rental
- File monthly TOT return + payment (due ~20th of following month)

**Cost:** No separate fee; 10–12% of nightly revenue paid to County  
**Timeline:** 1 week (TOT account setup)

---

### Step 6d: List Property on Hipcamp/Airbnb & Begin Bookings
**Action:** Create listing with professional photos, rates, availability calendar

**Contents:**
- Location description + site map
- Amenities (water access, parking, fire rings, etc.)
- House rules (quiet hours, number of guests, pet policy)
- Nightly rate (research comparables in Boulder Creek area)
- Booking links to Hipcamp/Airbnb

**Cost:** 3–10% commission (platform fee)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

**Expected result:** First bookings + nightly revenue.

# PART 2: APN 086-521-02 (RA Zoning) — New Primary Residence

## Overview

APN 086-521-02 (1.492 acres, RA zoning, 20604 Little Basin Rd) is the smaller parcel and ideal for a primary residence / access structure. RA zoning is permissive for single-family residential use; no CUP needed.

**Timeline:** 8–12 weeks (shorter than glamping CUP path; can run parallel).

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Design & Zoning Verification (Weeks 1–2)

### Step 1a: Schedule Pre-Application with County Planning (RA Zoning)
**Action:** Same call to 831-454-2702, add to your pre-app agenda:

**Request:**
- "Second parcel is APN 086-521-02 (1.49 acres, RA zoning). Planning a primary residence. What are residential density limits and setback requirements?"
- "Can primary residence be owner-occupied caretaker dwelling for the glamping operation on APN -14?"
- "What utilities are available: municipal water/sewer, or well/septic?"
- "Are there deed restrictions or easements on the parcel?"

**Cost:** Free (included in pre-app for APN -14)  
**Timeline:** Same meeting

---

### Step 1b: Verify Parcel Zoning & Utility Availability
**Action:** Online research + pre-app call results

**Zoning verification:**
- Confirm RA zoning allows single-family residence (it should)
- Note setback requirements (typical: 20–50 ft from road + property lines)
- Note lot coverage limits (typical: 25–50% for RA)
- Note height limits (typical: 35–45 ft)

**Utility check:**
- Is parcel on County water system? (If yes, building is simpler)
- Is parcel near municipal sewer? (Boulder Creek may have limited sewer; assume well/septic needed)
- Road frontage adequate for emergency vehicle access? (35 ft typical)

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** 1 day (online + pre-app call)

---

### Step 1c: Request "Proof of Zoning Compliance" Letter
**Action:** County Planning issues one-page letter confirming parcel zoning + allowed uses

**Use for:** Building permit application + (if filing STR for caretaker cottage) STR registration.

**Cost:** $0–50  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

---

## Phase 2: Site Survey & Preliminary Design (Weeks 3–6)

### Step 2a: Commission Topographic Site Survey
**Action:** Hire surveyor to produce topographic site plan showing:
- Parcel boundary (metes & bounds from deed)
- Existing structures (if any)
- Contours/elevations (assess building site flatness)
- Easements / utility locations
- Road frontage + access

**Cost:** $800–2,000  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks

**Why:** Identifies best building location, determines septic field feasibility, reveals slope/drainage issues.

---

### Step 2b: Commission Septic Feasibility Study
**Action:** Hire septic designer to conduct soil percolation test + layout

**Study scope:**
- Soil boring + percolation test (determines leaching rate)
- Septic tank size recommendation (for 4-bed residence: typically 1,250–1,500 gal)
- Drainfield sizing + location options
- Alternative systems (if standard septic infeasible: aerobic, sand filter, mound system)

**Cost:** $1,500–3,000  
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks

**Expected result:** Septic design report + site map showing tank/drainfield location (ready for building permit).

---

### Step 2c: Assess Water Supply Options
**Action:** Determine if well drilling is needed; if yes, hire well contractor for estimate

**Options:**
1. **Municipal water:** If available through County/local provider → simpler, no well needed
2. **Domestic well:** If required, typical yield assessment costs $500–1,500; drilling cost $3,000–6,000 depending on depth

**Action:**
- Call County Planning: "Is domestic well water available for APN 086-521-02?"
- If yes: Get County's well water quality testing requirements
- If no: Estimate municipal water extension cost (can be $5K–20K+ depending on distance)

**Cost:** $500–1,500 (feasibility); $3,000–6,000 (drilling)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks (feasibility)

---

### Step 2d: Preliminary House Design (Architect/Designer)
**Action:** Commission preliminary design showing:
- 3–4 bedroom house (typical primary residence + guest space for caretaker function)
- ~2,000–2,500 sq ft (adjust per budget)
- Site layout (parking, septic, well location, driveway)
- Utilities (electrical service, propane/natural gas vs. electric)
- Fire/life safety (egress windows, smoke detectors, accessible routes if applicable)

**Cost:** $3,000–8,000 (preliminary design / schematic design)  
**Timeline:** 3–4 weeks

**Why architect:** Ensures designs meet code, helps identify feasibility issues before building permit, can guide through approval process.

---

## Phase 3: Entitlements & Building Permits (Weeks 6–10)

### Step 3a: Prepare Building Permit Application Package
**Action:** Assemble per County Building & Safety Division requirements

**Application contents:**
1. **Building Permit Form** (SCC standard form)
2. **Site Plan** (from topographic survey)
   - Parcel boundary, contours, existing structures
   - Proposed house location + setbacks from property lines
   - Parking area (typically 2 spaces for residence)
   - Driveway (width, length, grade)
   - Septic tank + drainfield location
   - Well location (if applicable)
   - Utility lines (electrical, gas, water, septic, stormwater)

3. **Architectural Plans** (preliminary design)
   - Floor plan(s)
   - Elevations (4 sides of house)
   - Roof plan
   - Cross-sections (if complex topography)
   - Foundation plan (if needed for code review)
   - Electrical one-line diagram (service size, panel location)
   - Plumbing plan (showing septic connection)

4. **Septic Design** (from Step 2b study)
   - Septic tank sizing + location
   - Drainfield layout (setbacks from well, property line, surface water)
   - Soil test results
   - Designer certification

5. **Well Design** (if applicable, from Step 2c)
   - Well location + protection radius
   - Casing depth + grouting plan
   - Water quality test results

6. **Site Grading & Drainage Plan**
   - How surface water is managed (swales, detention, direction away from structures)
   - Erosion control (if site is hillside)

7. **Zoning Compliance Checklist**
   - Confirm setbacks met
   - Confirm lot coverage within limit
   - Confirm height within limit
   - Proof of zoning compliance letter (from Step 1c)

**Cost:** $200–500 (permit fee estimate; confirm with County)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks to assemble

---

### Step 3b: Pre-Submittal Review (Optional but Recommended)
**Action:** Contact County Building Dept: "Can we schedule a 30-min pre-submittal meeting to confirm our plans are on track?"

**What to review with Building Inspector:**
- Site plan (road access, parking, utilities, septic)
- Architectural design (setbacks, height, footprint)
- Septic design (County may have specific requirements)
- Fire safety (egress, address signage, driveway)

**Cost:** $0–100 (sometimes free, sometimes small fee)  
**Timeline:** 1 week (scheduling + meeting)

**Why:** Catches potential issues before formal submission; saves revision cycles.

---

### Step 3c: Submit Building Permit Application
**Action:** File application package with County Building & Safety Division

**Submission:**
- Original + 2–4 copies (confirm with Building Dept)
- Application fee (budget $300–800)
- Cover letter requesting completeness review within 10 days

**What to expect:**
- Intake review (1 week)
- Completeness determination (if complete → formal plan review begins)
- If incomplete → return list of missing items → you revise + resubmit

**Cost:** $300–800 (permit fee)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks completeness review

---

### Step 3d: Building Department Plan Review
**Action:** County Building Dept reviews plans for code compliance

**Review scope:**
- **Structural:** Foundation design, framing per IRC (International Residential Code)
- **Fire/Life Safety:** Egress windows (bedrooms), door sizes, smoke detectors, sprinklers (if required)
- **Electrical:** Service size, panel location, outlet spacing per NEC
- **Plumbing:** Septic connection, fixture count, vent stacks, water supply line protection
- **Accessibility:** If owner-occupied residence, minimal ADA requirements; caretaker use may trigger more
- **Site/Grading:** Stormwater management, driveway grade, erosion control
- **Energy Code:** California Title 24 compliance (insulation, HVAC, windows)

**Likely outcomes:**
- ✅ **Approved as submitted** (rare but possible)
- ✅ **Approved with minor corrections** (typical): 1–2 revision cycles, minor changes
- 🔄 **Approved with major revisions:** 2–3 revision cycles, significant redesign needed

**Cost:** $0 (included in permit fee)  
**Timeline:** 3–4 weeks (plan review)

---

### Step 3e: Revisions & Re-Submittal (If Needed)
**Action:** If Building Dept requests revisions:
1. Revise affected plans (architect updates drawings)
2. Respond to each comment in writing ("See revision on Sheet X.X")
3. Resubmit marked-up plans + revision letter

**Cost:** $0 (revision fee is included in original permit fee for most counties; double-check with SCC)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks per revision cycle

---

### Step 3f: Building Permit Issued & NTP (Notice to Proceed)
**Action:** County issues building permit + site-specific conditions

**Permit includes:**
- Permit number + expiration date (usually 1 year; renewable)
- Approved construction documents
- List of conditions (e.g., "Inspector approval required at each stage", "Weekly stormwater inspections if grading occurs during rainy season")
- Contact info for Building Inspector assigned to project

**Cost:** $0 (already paid)  
**Timeline:** 1 week (after final plan review approval)

---

## Phase 4: Construction & Inspections (Weeks 10–26)

### Step 4a: Pre-Construction Meeting
**Action:** Meet with Building Inspector before construction begins

**Agenda:**
- Review permit conditions
- Confirm inspection schedule (typical: foundation, framing, electrical/plumbing rough, final)
- Discuss site access (address signage for emergency vehicles)
- Clarify questions on approved plans

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** 1 week before breaking ground

---

### Step 4b: Site Preparation & Foundation Work
**Action:** Clear site, set up temporary access, begin excavation for foundation + septic/well

**Inspection triggers:**
- **Excavation inspection:** Before foundation concrete poured
- **Septic tank installation inspection:** Tank set + initial backfill, before drainfield begins
- **Well drilling inspection** (if applicable): Before casing grouted + finalized

**Building Inspector approval required** before moving to next phase.

**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks (site prep + foundation)

---

### Step 4c: Foundation & Framing
**Action:** Foundation concrete cured → structural framing (walls, roof)

**Inspection triggers:**
- **Foundation inspection:** Concrete cured, before backfill
- **Framing inspection:** Walls & roof structure complete, before sheathing/insulation

**Timeline:** 4–6 weeks

---

### Step 4d: Building Skin (Roofing, Exterior Walls, Windows/Doors)
**Action:** Weather-tight envelope (roof, siding, windows, doors installed)

**Inspection triggers:**
- **Weather-tight inspection:** May be required by County before interior work (protects from water damage during construction)

**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks

---

### Step 4e: Electrical & Plumbing Rough-In
**Action:** Electrical service installed; electrical/plumbing lines run in-wall; HVAC ducting installed

**Inspection triggers:**
- **Electrical rough inspection:** Wiring in walls, panel installed, before drywall
- **Plumbing rough inspection:** Water lines, drain lines, vents in place, before walls closed
- **HVAC rough inspection** (if forced-air system): Ductwork installed, before drywall

**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks

---

### Step 4f: Insulation, Drywall, Interior Finish
**Action:** Insulation, drywall, flooring, paint, cabinetry, fixtures installed

**Inspection triggers:**
- **Rough plumbing inspection** (if not done): Before drywall obscures lines
- **Rough electrical inspection** (if not done): Before drywall
- Typically no Building Inspector inspection during finish phase (unless issues flagged earlier)

**Timeline:** 4–6 weeks

---

### Step 4g: Final Inspections & Certificate of Occupancy
**Action:** Building Inspector conducts comprehensive final walkthrough

**Final inspection covers:**
- All required inspections completed with sign-offs
- Plans vs. built condition matches
- Code violations corrected
- All systems operational (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, septic, well)
- Address signage installed
- Parking area complete (if required)
- Grading/drainage swales functional

**If passed:** Building Dept issues Certificate of Occupancy (CO) or Permit Closure  
**If failed:** Deficiency list → correct items → request re-inspection

**Cost:** $0 (included in permit fee)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks (final inspection turnaround)

---

### Step 4h: Occupancy
**Action:** Once CO issued, residence is legally occupiable.

**Next steps:**
- Establish utilities (electric/gas account, water/septic service)
- Obtain occupancy certificate if needed for insurance/lender
- Residence can now function as primary home or caretaker dwelling

---

## Phase 5: Optional STR Registration for Caretaker Cottage (Weeks 26–28)

### Step 5a: Decide on Caretaker Rental Status
**Action:** Determine if residence should be registered as STR for "guest house" / "caretaker lodging" revenue

**Options:**
1. **Owner-occupied primary residence:** No STR registration needed
2. **Caretaker-occupied (live-in manager):** No STR registration (long-term resident)
3. **Guest house / Airbnb:** STR registration required (nightly transient guests)

**Decision factors:**
- Is residence primary income or secondary use?
- Will it be staffed with a live-in caretaker (not STR)?
- Or will it be rented nightly when not occupied by owner (STR)?

**Cost:** $0 (decision only)  
**Timeline:** 1 day

---

### Step 5b: File STR Registration (If Guest House Model)
**Action:** If choosing guest house model:

**Submit to SCC Assessor–Clerk Recorder:**
- Ownership proof
- Building permit closure / Certificate of Occupancy
- Insurance certificate (liability)
- Operational plan (nightly rate, occupancy limit = 4–6 guests typical for 3-bed house)
- Owner contact info

**Cost:** $150–300 (annual STR registration)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks

**TOT obligation:** Collect 10–12% nightly TOT; remit monthly to County.

---

### Step 5c: List on Hipcamp/Airbnb (If Guest House Model)
**Action:** Create professional listing with photos, rates, availability

**Cost:** 3–10% commission per booking  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks
