# Septic System FAQ for Glamping STR

## How Long Would a Single Person Take to Fill a Holding Tank?

### Wastewater Generation Estimates
For planning purposes, use these conservative estimates:

**Per Person Per Day (Glamping Context):**
- Toilet flushing: 20-40 gallons (assuming 1.6-3.5 gpf, 5-8 flushes/day)
- Showering: 10-25 gallons (5-minute shower at 2-5 gpm)
- Sink use (handwashing, brushing teeth): 2-5 gallons
- Cooking/cleaning: 2-5 gallons
- **Total per person/day**: 34-75 gallons (use 50 gallons for conservative planning)

### Holding Tank Fill Time Calculation
**Formula:** `Days to Fill = Tank Size (gallons) ÷ (Daily Usage × Number of Occupants)`

### Examples for Glamping Units:
| Tank Size | Single Person | 2 Persons | 4 Persons | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| 1,000 gal | 20 days | 10 days | 5 days | Minimum practical size |
| 1,500 gal | 30 days | 15 days | 7-8 days | Common for small cabins |
| 2,000 gal | 40 days | 20 days | 10 days | Good for 2-glamping unit cluster |
| 2,500 gal | 50 days | 25 days | 12-13 days | Larger capacity |
| 3,000 gal | 60 days | 30 days | 15 days | For higher usage or less frequent pumping |

### Real-World Glamping Considerations:
1. **Occupancy Variability**: Not all units occupied every day
2. **Water Conservation**: Glamping guests often use less water than at home
3. **Peak Usage**: Weekends/holidays may see higher occupancy
4. **Conservative Planning**: Design for 2-person average occupancy even if units sleep more

**Practical Recommendation:** 
- For a single glamping unit (max 4 occupants): 1,500-2,000 gallon tank
- Expected fill time with 2-person avg occupancy: 15-20 days
- Plan pumping schedule: Every 10-14 days to maintain safety margin
- With alarm system: Set alarm at 75% capacity for automatic warning

## What is Required for a Perc (Percolation) Test?

### Purpose of Perc Test
Measures how quickly water drains through soil to determine if a conventional septic leach field will function properly. Results determine leach field size requirements.

### Santa Cruz County Requirements (Typical):
1. **Who Can Perform**: Must be conducted by:
   - Licensed Civil Engineer
   - Registered Environmental Health Specialist (REHS)
   - Licensed Soil Scientist
   - *Generally NOT allowed for homeowners to perform themselves for permitting purposes*

2. **Test Procedure**:
   - Dig test holes to required depth (typically 2-5 feet)
   - Pre-soak soil for 4+ hours (or overnight)
   - Measure time for water to drop a specific distance (usually 6 inches)
   - Multiple tests at different locations and depths
   - Calculate percolation rate in minutes per inch (MPI)

3. **Acceptable Perc Rates** (varies by jurisdiction):
   - Typically 1-60 MPI for conventional systems
   - <1 MPI: Too slow (may need alternative system)
   - >60 MPI: Too fast (may need liner or alternative system)
   - *Santa Cruz County specific rates should be confirmed with Environmental Health*

4. **Number of Tests Required**:
   - Usually 3-6 test holes per proposed leach field area
   - Tests at different depths and locations
   - Both wet season and dry season may be required

5. **Documentation Required**:
   - Soil profile descriptions at each test hole
   - Water stabilization times
   - Perc rate calculations
   - Site plan showing test locations
   - Professional signature and stamp

### Can You Perform It Yourself?
**For Informal Planning Only:**
- You can dig holes and observe drainage for your own understanding
- This helps you know if you likely HAVE or DON'T HAVE suitable soil
- **BUT**: Results from DIY tests are NOT acceptable for permit applications

**For Official Purposes:**
- Must be performed by licensed professional
- County will reject DIY perc test results
- Professional carries liability insurance for accuracy
- Ensures proper technique and interpretation

### Cost & Timing:
- **Cost**: $300-$800+ depending on number of holes and site accessibility
- **Timing**: Best performed during wet season (when soil is saturated)
- **Validity**: Typically 2-5 years (check with county)

## Regulations for Installing a Leach Field (Drainfield)

### Key Regulatory Requirements (Santa Cruz County):

#### 1. **Permitting Process**
- **Application**: Submit to Santa Cruz County Environmental Health Services
- **Plans Required**: 
  - Site plan showing property boundaries, structures, wells, waterways, etc.
  - Detailed septic system design (tank size, leach field layout, dimensions)
  - Perc test results and soil evaluation
  - Construction details and specifications
- **Review Time**: Typically 2-4 weeks
- **Fees**: $500-$1,500+ depending on system complexity

#### 2. **Setback Requirements** (Typical - verify with county):
- From wells: 50-100 feet
- From property lines: 5-10 feet
- From waterways (creeks, ponds): 50-100 feet
- From cut banks or steep slopes: varies with height
- From foundations: 5-10 feet
- From swimming pools: varies
- From easements/rights-of-way: varies

#### 3. **Design Requirements**:
- **Leach Field Sizing**: Based on:
  - Number of bedrooms (or fixture units for commercial)
  - Perc test results (slower soil = larger field)
  - Daily wastewater flow estimates
- **Trench Dimensions**:
  - Width: Typically 18-36 inches
  - Depth: Typically 18-36 inches below surface
  - Length: Calculated based on loading rate
  - Spacing between trenches: Typically 6 feet (center-to-center)
- **Materials**:
  - Perforated pipe (typically 4-inch diameter)
  - Gravel or crushed stone bedding (washed, 3/4"-2.5" size)
  - Geotextile fabric to cover gravel (prevents soil intrusion)
  - Native soil backfill (top 6-12 inches)

#### 4. **Installation Requirements**:
- **Inspections Required**:
  - Open hole/trench inspection (before gravel placement)
  - Final cover inspection (after backfill)
- **Workmanship**:
  - Trenches must be level (±2 inches over 100 feet)
  - Proper pipe slope (typically 1/4 inch per foot toward tank)
  - Gravel must be clean and properly graded
  - Fabric must be installed correctly to prevent clogging
- **Protection**:
  - No heavy equipment over leach field during or after installation
  - No planting of trees or shrubs with invasive roots
  - No driving or parking vehicles over the field

#### 5. **Alternative When Perc Test Fails**:
If soil doesn't perc well enough for conventional leach field:
- **Mound System**: Elevated sand mound with pressurized distribution
- **Sand Filter**: Intermittent sand filter followed by discharge
- **Aerobic Treatment Unit (ATU)**: Adds oxygen to improve treatment
- **Drip Distribution**: Uses small diameter tubing for precise dosing
- **Holding Tank**: As discussed in separate document (haul-away)

### DIY Limitations & Risks:
While you can certainly:
- Dig test holes for personal education
- Research regulations and best practices
- Prepare questions for professionals
- Participate in planning discussions

**You CANNOT legally:**
- Perform official perc tests for permit submission
- Design a septic system for permit submission (unless you're a licensed engineer/designer)
- Install a septic system that requires a permit without proper licensing (in most jurisdictions)
- Submit DIY test results or designs to the county for approval

### Recommended Approach for Your Glamping Project:

1. **Initial Research**:
   - Use Web Soil Survey (NRCS) to get preliminary soil data
   - Observe existing vegetation and site conditions
   - Talk to neighbors about their septic experiences

2. **Professional Consultation**:
   - Hire a licensed septic designer or civil engineer familiar with Santa Cruz County
   - Have them conduct official perc test and soil evaluation
   - Get preliminary feasibility assessment before major investment

3. **Design & Permitting**:
   - Work with professional to create code-compliant plans
   - Submit to Environmental Health for review
   - Address any comments or requested modifications
   - Obtain permit before any installation begins

4. **Installation**:
   - Hire licensed septic installer
   - Ensure all required inspections are scheduled and passed
   - Obtain final approval and "as-built" documentation

5. **Documentation for Future**:
   - Keep all permits, designs, inspection reports
   - Record exact location of tank and leach field (GPS coordinates helpful)
   - Note maintenance schedule and pumping records

### Cost Implications:
- **Perc Test & Design**: $1,000-$3,000+
- **Installation (Conventional)**: $5,000-$15,000+ (depends on size and site)
- **Alternative Systems**: Often 1.5-3x conventional cost
- **Permitting Fees**: $500-$1,500+

### Bottom Line:
While understanding the process is valuable and you should absolutely educate yourself, **official perc tests, septic designs, and installations for permitted systems must be handled by licensed professionals** in Santa Cruz County to ensure compliance, functionality, and protection of public health and groundwater.
