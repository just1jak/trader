## Overview: When STR Registration is Required

**STR registration** is mandatory for any property rented to transient guests (individuals not related to owner) for periods **less than 30 consecutive days**. In the glamping context, nightly rentals of campsites or structures trigger STR registration.

**Applies to both parcels:**
- **APN 086-631-14 (SU zoning):** Any glamping tent/site rented nightly → STR registration required
- **APN 086-521-02 (RA zoning):** If caretaker cottage or guest structure rented nightly → STR registration required

---

## California State Requirements (Government Code §27000+)

### Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT)
- **Mandatory on ALL transient lodging** (hotel, B&B, glamping, Airbnb, Hipcamp, vacation rental)
- **Rate:** 4–15% of nightly rental rate (SCC typically 10–12%)
- **Remittance:** Monthly to County Assessor–Clerk Recorder
- **Penalties:** Late payment = 10% surcharge + interest (1.5% per month)

### State Fire Marshal Compliance (Building Code §304.3)
- **Short-term rental facilities must comply with California Building Code Division 17 (Enforcement)**
- **Life safety requirements:**
  - Hard-wired smoke detectors (every bedroom + hallway)
  - Carbon monoxide detectors (if fuel-burning appliances)
  - Fire extinguishers (accessible locations)
  - Emergency egress routes (min 2 exits, clearly marked)
- **For glamping tents/structures:** Temporary or permanent structure classification determines code tier

## Santa Cruz County Specific Requirements

### Local STR Registration (SCC Ordinance — Chapter 13.10+ or local chapter)
**Estimated requirements (typical CA county pattern):**

| Requirement | Details | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **STR Registration Certificate** | Property-specific permit allowing transient lodging | $100–300/yr | 2–3 weeks intake |
| **Business Tax Certificate** | General business registration (if not already held) | $50–150 | 1 week |
| **Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) Account** | Monthly reporting to County Assessor–Clerk Recorder | $0 (but 10–12% of revenue remitted monthly) | 1 week |
| **Proof of Zoning Compliance** | Demonstration that STR use is permitted under parcel zoning | $0 (cert from County Planning) | 1–2 weeks |
| **Insurance (Liability + Host Protection)** | General liability $300K–$1M minimum | $500–1,500/yr | Immediate |

### APN-Specific Zoning Compliance

#### **APN 086-631-14 (SU Zoning)**
- **STR Status:** **Conditional Use** — requires CUP (see REGULATORY_LOG.md)
- **Implication:** STR registration + CUP approval both required before first nightly rental
- **Timeline impact:** CUP (14–25 weeks) gates STR registration approval

#### **APN 086-521-02 (RA Zoning)**
- **STR Status:** Likely **Permitted** (RA typically allows single-family rentals + caretaker uses)
- **Implication:** STR registration may be faster (no CUP needed for owner-occupied or caretaker use)
- **Caveat:** Must confirm with County Planning; some RA zoning restricts transient use

### Glamping-Specific Nuances
- **Canvas tents / Bell tents:** Classified as **temporary structures** (typically exempt from building permits if <180 days/year) but **still trigger STR registration + TOT**
- **Platforms / Decks:** If permanent (attached to ground, utilities), require building permits + STR registration
- **Primitive camping (Hipcamp/Airbnb "private land sharing"):** Often claimed as **non-commercial** (guests bring own shelter), but if property collects nightly fees → STR registration still applies
- **Organized Camp Permit (SCC 7.44):** If operating >5 campsites, Camp Permit may supersede STR registration (mutual exclusivity needs clarification)

## Required Documents for STR Registration

1. **Parcel Ownership Proof** (deed, title report, or recorded deed)
2. **Zoning Compliance Certificate** (from SCC Planning Dept)
3. **Proof of Insurance** (liability policy covering STR/transient use)
4. **Site Plan** (if structures present; otherwise brief description of camping layout)
5. **Owner Contact + Emergency Contact** (name, address, phone, email)
6. **Representation of Occupancy Limits** (max # of guests per night, max # of structures)

## Timeline for STR Registration

### Best Case (APN 086-521-02, RA zoning, no CUP needed)
- Pre-application: 1–2 weeks
- Zoning Compliance cert: 1 week
- Insurance obtained: 1 week
- STR registration intake + processing: 1–2 weeks
- **Total: 3–5 weeks** (can overlap with other tasks)

### Worst Case (APN 086-631-14, SU zoning, CUP required)
- CUP approval: 14–25 weeks (gates everything)
- Post-CUP Zoning Compliance cert: 1 week
- Insurance + STR registration: 2–3 weeks
- **Total: 14–25 weeks** (CUP is the driver)

## Key Decision Points

### 1. Which Parcel First?
- **Strategy:** Register APN 086-521-02 (RA zoning) first while CUP (14–25 weeks) runs on APN 086-631-14
- **Rationale:** RA likely has faster approval; generates initial revenue during CUP wait

### 2. Primitive vs. Glamping Liability
- **Primitive (tent camping only):** Liability policy ~$500–800/yr
- **Glamping (structures + amenities):** Liability policy ~$1,000–1,500/yr
- **Recommendation:** Obtain higher coverage upfront; cheaper to maintain than upgrade mid-season

### 3. Organized Camp Permit vs. STR Registration
- **If >5 campsites:** Organized Camp Permit (SCC 7.44) may supersede or run parallel to STR registration
- **Clarification needed:** Call County Planning to confirm mutual exclusivity
- **Impact:** Could reduce # of permits needed, but timing / cost implications TBD
