## SU Zoning Analysis (APN 086-631-14)

**Zoning Code:** SCC Chapter 13.20 (Special Use District)

**Allowed Uses (without CUP):**
- Single-family residence
- Agriculture (forestry, general farm use)

**Conditional Uses (require CUP per Chapter 13.28):**
- Campgrounds and RV parks
- Guest ranches and glamping operations
- Organized camps (also need SCC 7.44 Organized Camp Permit if >5 campsites)
- Short-term rentals (require both CUP and STR registration)

**Key Restrictions for Glamping on SU:**
- **ALL commercial/quasi-commercial uses require Conditional Use Permit** issued by Hearing Examiner
- CEQA environmental review mandatory for commercial operations
- Must satisfy SCC Chapter 13.28 CUP approval standards (land use compatibility, public welfare, etc.)
- Building permits required for any permanent/semi-permanent structures
- Coastal Commission jurisdiction may apply (parcel location-dependent)

**Permit Hierarchy for Glamping on SU:**
1. Land Use Consistency determination (General Plan alignment)
2. Conditional Use Permit application + Hearing Examiner approval
3. Organized Camp Permit (if >5 campsites, per SCC 7.44)
4. Building + Fire + Health permits for improvements
5. Coastal Commission review (if applicable)

**Strategic Implications:**
- SU zoning is more restrictive than RA for campground/glamping uses
- CUP requirement adds 3-6 months to approval timeline and $2-5K in application/hearing costs
- Success depends on Hearing Examiner finding public benefit + compatibility with SU district character

**Researched:** 2026-05-10 (Burndown Tick)

### CEQA Determination: **Discretionary Project — Negative Declaration (ND) Expected**

**Project Classification:**
- Use change from unimproved land → commercial glamping/campground = **discretionary project**
- Triggers CEQA under Public Resources Code §21080(b)(1)
- Lead Agency: Santa Cruz County Planning Department

**Likely Environmental Impacts (Initial Study triggers):**
1. **Land Use Compatibility** — changes unimproved mountain land to commercial lodging
2. **Traffic/Access** — nightly guest arrivals on rural road network
3. **Utilities & Infrastructure** — septic system, water, electrical, wastewater
4. **Biological Resources** — potential habitat disturbance (SCC Mountains are forested)
5. **Visual Impact** — structures on scenic mountain land
6. **Noise** — guest activity in quiet rural area

**CEQA Pathway (Projected):**
- County issues **Notice of Preparation (NOP)** → 30-day public comment
- Applicant prepares **Initial Study (IS)** → County reviews for significant impacts
- If no significant unavoidable impacts found → **Negative Declaration (ND)** → mitigations may be required
- If significant impacts present → **Environmental Impact Report (EIR)** required

**Timeline Impact:** ND adds 6–10 weeks to entitlement schedule; EIR adds 3–6 months

**Mitigation Measures Likely Required:**
- Biological surveys (endangered species, habitat assessment)
- Traffic study (peak season guest load analysis)
- Visual/aesthetic analysis (mountain views, setback requirements)
- Stormwater management plan (hillside erosion control)
- Noise control measures (quiet hours, sound insulation)

## Permit Fees & Timelines (CUP + Organized Camp Permit)
**Researched:** 2026-05-11 (Burndown Tick)

### Conditional Use Permit (CUP) — For Glamping on SU Zoning

**Estimated Fees (SCC typical):**
- Application filing fee: **$2,000–4,000**
- Hearing Examiner appearance fee: **$500–1,500** (per hearing)
- Environmental review coordination: **$1,000–3,000** (Initial Study prep by applicant; County review fee ~$500–1,000)
- **Total CUP cost: $3,500–8,500**

*Source: SCC Chapter 13.28 fee schedule (exact amounts require manual PDF download from sccgov.org/Sites/Planning/)*

**Estimated Timeline:**
- Application intake & completeness review: **2–4 weeks**
- Environmental review (Negative Declaration assumed): **6–10 weeks**
- Hearing Examiner scheduling & hearing: **4–8 weeks post-NOP clearance**
- Decision & permit issuance: **2–3 weeks post-hearing**
- **Total CUP timeline: 14–25 weeks (~3–6 months)**

**Key Timeline Driver:** Hearing Examiner workload varies; SCC Planning Dept can estimate current docket at intake.

### Organized Camp Permit (SCC 7.44) — For >5 Campsites

**Estimated Fees (SCC typical):**
- Permit application fee: **$300–600**
- Inspection fees (fire + health): **$150–300 per inspection** (usually 2–3 inspections)
- Annual renewal: **$200–400**
- **Total initial cost: $650–1,300**

*Source: SCC Chapter 7.44 fee schedule (exact amounts require manual lookup)*

**Estimated Timeline:**
- Application intake: **1–2 weeks**
- Fire Department inspection (life safety, egress, water): **2–4 weeks**
- Health Department review (sanitation, utilities): **2–4 weeks**
- Final approval & permit issuance: **1–2 weeks**
- **Total camp permit timeline: 6–12 weeks (~1.5–3 months)**

**Key Timeline Driver:** Coordinated multi-department review (fire + health + County Planning).

### Combined Entitlement Path

**Sequence:**
1. CUP application filed → runs parallel with Organized Camp Permit application
2. Environmental review (CEQA) completes → Hearing Examiner receives CUP application
3. Hearing Examiner issues CUP decision
4. Camp Permit issuance (generally same or next business day after CUP approved)

**Critical Path:** CUP drives timeline (14–25 weeks); Camp Permit (6–12 weeks) largely concurrent.

**Total estimated entitlement time: 4–7 months** (from application to both permits in hand).
