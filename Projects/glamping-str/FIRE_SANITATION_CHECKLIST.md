# Fire-Safety & Sanitation Clearance Criteria
**Glamping-STR Project | APN 086-631-14 (Primary) + 086-521-02 (Access)**

---

## Overview

Fire and sanitation clearances are required for any campground/glamping operation in SCC under:
- **Fire Code:** California Fire Code (CFC, adopted by SCC) + SCC County Fire Marshal authority
- **Health Code:** SCC Chapter 7.44 (Organized Camp Permit), County Health Department
- **Sanitation:** SCC Chapter 5 (Environmental Health), SCC Hazardous Waste Division

These are prerequisites for **Organized Camp Permit issuance** (required if >5 campsites). Fire and Health Departments typically issue clearances in tandem (2–3 coordinated inspections during permit application).

| Phase | Structures | Utilities | Fire/Health Clearance? | Primary Concern |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Phase 1: Primitive** (tents, no amenities) | None | None | **NO** — Private Land Sharing exempt | Liability insurance only |
| **Phase 2: Enhanced Primitive** (canvas tents, fire rings) | Portable, <6 mo | Basic water | **PARTIAL** — Safety plan only | Fire rings, guest safety briefing |

| **Phase 4: Full Glamping** (platforms, utilities) | Permanent, >6 mo | Full utilities | **YES** — Full Organized Camp Permit | All of Phase 3 + building code compliance |

## Fire Safety Checklist (SCC Fire Marshal Authority)

### 1. Site Access & Life Safety
- [ ] **Primary access road:** Minimum 12 feet wide, maintained to accommodate fire truck (35 ft length, 10.5 ft height clearance)
- [ ] **Turnaround area:** Adequate for fire apparatus to turn around or back-in capability (minimum 30 ft × 30 ft cleared area, or road width sufficient for 2-way traffic)
- [ ] **Road markings:** Address number clearly visible from main road (minimum 4" letters, reflective)
- [ ] **Signage:** "No Parking" at access gates/turnouts; "Campground Entrance" sign at entry
- [ ] **Lot coverage (campground):** Max 30% of site area (preserves defensible space + emergency egress)
- [ ] **Gate locks:** Must allow emergency vehicle access (combination lock or Knox box, not chain-locked)

### 2. Defensible Space (Wildfire Mitigation)
*Required if parcel is in SCC High Fire Hazard Severity Zone (HFHSZ); confirm with Fire Marshal.*

**Zone 0 (Immediate, 0–5 ft from structures):**
- [ ] Remove dead/dying vegetation, branches within 10 ft of roofs
- [ ] Clear pine needles, leaves from gutters and roof surfaces
- [ ] Remove vines from structures
- [ ] Space trees minimum 12 ft apart (crown to crown)
- [ ] Remove lower branches of trees (minimum 8–10 ft clearance from ground)
- [ ] Maintain grass at <4" height

**Zone 1 (Intermediate, 5–30 ft from structures):**
- [ ] Thin trees; minimum 12 ft spacing (crown to crown)
- [ ] Remove ladder fuels (branches, shrubs within 6–10 ft of tree trunks)
- [ ] Clear fallen branches, dead wood piles

**Zone 2 (Extended, 30–100+ ft from structures):**
- [ ] Remove diseased, dead, or dying trees
- [ ] Space living trees minimum 20 ft apart
- [ ] Limit chaparral density (typical SCC mountain vegetation)

**Vegetation Management Plan:** Provided to Fire Marshal at pre-construction inspection. Ongoing maintenance = guest responsibility (or include in rental terms).

### 3. Water Supply for Fire (Campground >5 sites)
- [ ] **Static water supply:** Minimum 2,500 gallons on-site (tank, pond, or hydrant connection)
  - Tank must be accessible to fire truck (14 ft wide lane minimum)
  - Color: white or bright (visible to Fire Marshal)
  - Valve: minimum 2.5" opening for fire hose connection
- [ ] **Alternative:** Direct hydrant connection to County water system (requires SCC Public Works approval)
- [ ] **Labeling:** Water supply clearly marked with "WATER FOR FIRE" sign + volume
- [ ] **Year-round availability:** Mechanism to prevent freezing (SCC mountainous = winter freeze risk)

*Phase 1–2 (primitive) exemption: Private Land Sharing uses may not require on-site water tanks (verify with Fire Marshal).*

### 4. Fire Extinguishers & Equipment
- [ ] **In common areas:** Type ABC fire extinguishers (minimum 10 lbs each), minimum 2 units
  - Mounted on walls, visible, accessible
  - Inspection tag current (annual inspection required)
- [ ] **At propane/fuel storage:** Type ABC extinguishers immediately adjacent
- [ ] **Guest tents/structures:** Fire extinguisher accessible within 75 ft

### 5. Emergency Response & Signage
- [ ] **Emergency contact number:** Permanent posting at site entrance + in office
- [ ] **911 address signage:** Large, reflective letters at entry (also on mailbox/gate)
- [ ] **Site map:** Posted at entry showing emergency assembly point, water supply, extinguisher locations
- [ ] **Phone reception:** Cell coverage verified on-site; if poor, satellite phone or emergency alert system required
- [ ] **Evacuation plan:** Written and posted; guests briefed upon arrival

### 6. Propane & Fuel Storage (if applicable)
- [ ] **Propane tank location:** Minimum 10 ft from structures, utility lines, electrical equipment
- [ ] **Tank size limit:** If >500 lbs aggregate, requires SCC Building & Fire approval + inspection
- [ ] **Labeling:** "PROPANE" sign visible; MSDS posted nearby
- [ ] **Vent relief:** Accessible, free of obstruction
- [ ] **Annual inspection:** SCC fire marshal or certified propane company

### 7. Electrical Safety (If permanent wiring installed)
- [ ] **Inspection by licensed electrician:** SCC requires inspection of any structures >6 months
- [ ] **Permit:** Building permit required for permanent electrical work
- [ ] **Ground fault protection:** GFCI outlets in areas near water (bathhouses, kitchens)
- [ ] **Fire suppression integration:** Any permanent structures with electrical loads ≥30A must comply with National Electrical Code (NEC)

## Sanitation & Health Checklist (SCC Health Department / Chapter 7.44)

### 1. Water Supply (Potable & Non-Potable)
**Potable Water (drinking, cooking, washing):**
- [ ] **Source verification:** Well, county system, or approved hauled water
- [ ] **Well test (if on-site well):**
  - Annual bacteriological (coliform) test required
  - Test report provided to Health Dept
  - SCC requires lab approval (certified water testing lab)
- [ ] **County water connection:** If connected, meter installed and records kept
- [ ] **Water storage tank (if applicable):**
  - Capacity adequate for 50+ guests (typically 500–5,000 gal depending on density)
  - Tank material: food-grade plastic or stainless steel (no metal tanks that rust)
  - Covered, labeled "POTABLE WATER"
  - Access for cleaning/inspection (cleanout port minimum 4" diameter)
  - Chlorination or UV treatment system (if well or surface source)
- [ ] **Distribution:** Underground piping preferred (or above-ground in frost-protected areas)
- [ ] **Pressure & flow:** Minimum 20 PSI maintained during peak use; >2 GPM at furthest tap

**Non-Potable Water (fire suppression, landscaping, toilet flushing):**
- [ ] **Source:** Rainwater cistern, reclaimed water, or designated non-potable line
- [ ] **Labeling:** "RECLAIMED WATER — NOT FOR DRINKING" visible on all outlets
- [ ] **Segregation:** Separate piping from potable system; no cross-connections (Health Dept inspects)

### 2. Wastewater & Sewage
**Option A: Septic System (Primary for rural SCC)**
- [ ] **System design:** Engineered septic system per SCC Hazardous Waste Division specs
  - Septic tank: Minimum 1,500 gal for 10–15 people (add 500 gal per 10 additional guests)
  - Leach field: Design percolation test (required before system sizing)
  - Soil evaluation: SCC Health Dept review of soil type, groundwater depth, setbacks
- [ ] **Setbacks from property lines & wells:**
  - 50 ft from domestic wells (SCC requirement)
  - 100 ft from surface water (streams, lakes)
  - 25 ft from property line (neighbor protection)
  - 100 ft from underground potable water source
- [ ] **Septic tank maintenance:**
  - Pumped every 3–5 years (SCC mandates documentation)
  - Inspection port accessible for Health Dept pumper trucks
  - No disposal of non-degradable materials (wipes, paper towels, grease)
- [ ] **Advanced treatment:** If on-site composting toilets used, system must still have backup septic or approved alternative (see below)

**Option B: Composting Toilets (Phase 2–3 compatible)**
- [ ] **Model approved:** SCC Health Dept pre-approval required (contact for approved models)
  - Waterless, aerobic, or low-water designs acceptable
  - Self-contained or central processing
- [ ] **Capacity:** Sized for expected guest load + retention time (typically 10–30 days)
- [ ] **Installation:** Per manufacturer specs; accessible for maintenance
- [ ] **Operational plan:** Maintenance protocol, compost disposal (off-site licensed facility or on-site composting with Health Dept approval)
- [ ] **Greywater integration:** Separate from toilet effluent (hand-washing, shower water via biofilter or treatment system)
- [ ] **Permit requirement:** Even composting toilets require Health Dept approval as primary sanitation system; backup septic may be required for emergency overflow

**Option C: Portable/Removable Toilets (Phase 1 only, temporary)**
- [ ] **Licensed service:** Contracted with SCC-approved portable toilet company
- [ ] **Frequency:** Pumped minimum 2x weekly during guest season
- [ ] **Signage:** "Portable Unit — Health Dept Approved" on each unit
- [ ] **Documentation:** Service logs provided to Health Dept upon inspection

### 3. Greywater (Shower, Sink, Laundry)
- [ ] **Option A: Separate treatment system**
  - Greywater septic tank (500–1,500 gal depending on volume)
  - Leach field dedicated to greywater only (meets reduced standard per SCC regs)
  - Setbacks: 50 ft from wells, 25 ft from property lines
- [ ] **Option B: Dispersal to landscaping**
  - Subsurface drip irrigation system (approved by Health Dept)
  - No spray irrigation (public health hazard)
  - Labeled area: "Reclaimed Water Irrigation — Keep Out"
- [ ] **Backup: Hauled water removal**
  - If no on-site treatment, greywater collected in tanks + pumped by licensed contractor
  - Service logs provided to Health Dept

### 4. Garbage & Waste Management
- [ ] **Solid waste storage:**
  - Dumpster or refuse bins with tight-fitting lids
  - Stored minimum 100 ft from campground (or per SCC zoning)
  - Regular pickup (minimum weekly during guest season)
  - Bear-proof containers (SCC requirement in wildlife zones; check w/ Zoning)
- [ ] **Recycling:** Separate bins for compost, glass, plastic, paper (SCC encourages; not mandatory)
- [ ] **Guest briefing:** Instructions for proper waste disposal posted in each tent/cabin
- [ ] **Hazardous waste:** Propane, pesticides, batteries collected separately; no on-site burning

### 5. Food Service (If any meals provided beyond self-catering)
- [ ] **Permit type:** If commercial kitchen on-site, Food Service Permit required (separate from Organized Camp Permit)
  - Equipment: Grease trap (if fryer used), 3-compartment sink, refrigeration
  - Staffing: Food handler card for any staff preparing food
  - Inspection: Monthly or quarterly Health Dept visits
- [ ] **No food service:** If guests self-cater only, this section is waived (most glamping models)

### 6. Hygiene Facilities
- [ ] **Handwashing:**
  - Minimum 2 hand-washing stations (soap, paper towels, hot water)
  - Accessible from toilet/kitchen areas
  - Signage: "Hand Washing Required"
- [ ] **Showers/Bathing:**
  - If shower facilities provided, min 1 shower per 15 guests (SCC standard)
  - Hot water temperature control (120°F max to prevent scalding)
  - Drainage to septic or approved treatment system
  - Cleaning/disinfection protocol posted
- [ ] **Laundry:** If provided, separate washer/dryer hook-ups; no public area for laundry (prevents contamination)

### 7. Health Inspection & Clearance
- [ ] **Pre-inspection meeting:** Health Inspector walk-through 2–4 weeks before opening
  - Identifies final issues before formal inspection
  - Confirms water supply, septic, waste management
- [ ] **Formal inspection:** Conducted during Organized Camp Permit application
  - Typically 1–2 inspections (initial + final clearance)
  - Corrections must be completed before permit issuance
- [ ] **Documentation required:**
  - Water testing results (potable water)
  - Septic design (engineer stamp) or composting toilet approval
  - Waste management contract (dumpster service)
  - Food service permit (if applicable)
  - Staff names + Food Handler cards (if meals provided)
- [ ] **Annual renewal:** Health Dept re-inspects for permit renewal (typically annual for Organized Camps >10 sites)

## Phase-by-Phase Implementation

### Phase 1: Primitive Camping (Private Land Sharing)
**Fire-Safety Requirements:**
- [ ] Address signage (visible from road)
- [ ] Gate locks (allow emergency access)
- [ ] Evacuation plan (posted at entry, verbal briefing to guests)
- [ ] Emergency contact number (posted + guest confirmation)

**Sanitation Requirements:**
- [ ] Portable toilet service (weekly pickup minimum) — NO Organized Camp Permit needed
- [ ] Trash bins with lids (bear-proof if needed)
- [ ] Hand-washing station (5-gallon water + soap)
- [ ] Liability waiver (guests assume risk; private land exemption)

**Health Clearance:** None required for Private Land Sharing model (exempt under California recreational use immunity).

---

### Phase 2: Enhanced Primitive (Bell Tents + Fire Rings)
**New Fire-Safety:**
- [ ] Fire ring/fire pit area (minimum 15 ft from nearest structure)
  - Marked with rocks or logs (visible perimeter)
  - Spade/water bucket immediately adjacent
  - Guest safety briefing: "No unattended fires"
- [ ] Fire extinguishers: 2x Type ABC (10 lbs) at common areas
- [ ] Water supply plan: Specify bucket brigade or hand-pump option (no tank required)

**New Sanitation:**
- [ ] Upgrade to larger portable toilet (or add second unit if >5 guests/day)
- [ ] Hand-washing upgrade: Pedal-pump or gravity-fed hand station + soap + paper towels
- [ ] Trash upgrade: Larger bin + more frequent service (2x weekly)

**Health Clearance:** Pre-inspection with Health Dept (informal) — confirm portable toilet adequacy + water supply sanity check.

---

### Phase 3: Low-Impact Utilities (Composting Toilets + Solar + Shelters)
**Full Fire Safety (Formal Inspection Required):**
- [ ] Defensible space assessment (SCC Fire Marshal site visit)
  - Document existing vegetation + planned clearing
  - Vegetation management plan (annual maintenance schedule)
- [ ] Water supply for fire: Install 2,500-gal static tank + hose connection
- [ ] Electrical (if solar): Licensed electrician inspection + building permit for any roofed structures >6 months
- [ ] Shelter structures: <30% lot coverage; non-permanent (removable in <6 months) per Fire Code

**Full Sanitation (Formal Inspection Required):**
- [ ] Septic system: Licensed engineer design + SCC Hazardous Waste approval + installation inspection
  - OR pre-approved composting toilet model + Health Dept acceptance
- [ ] Potable water supply: Well test results (coliform + bacterial) from SCC-certified lab
  - OR County water connection + meter
- [ ] Greywater system: Subsurface drip or separate greywater septic (see options above)
- [ ] First Organized Camp Permit application filed

**Health Clearance:** Full pre-construction inspection (Health + Fire Marshal); final clearance before permit issuance.

---

### Phase 4: Full Glamping (Permanent Structures + Professional Utilities)
**Building Code Compliance (Beyond Fire Safety):**
- [ ] Roofed platforms/structures: Permanent → Building Permit required per SCC Chapter 16
  - Engineer design, grading plan, foundation specs
  - Electrical: Licensed electrician inspection + NEC code compliance
  - Plumbing: Licensed plumber inspection + SCC standards
- [ ] Utility infrastructure: On-site electrical service (if >100A), boiler/HVAC systems, propane tank sizing
- [ ] Accessibility: ADA compliance review (if public gathering space > certain square footage)

**Full Sanitation Upgrade:**
- [ ] Septic system: Upsized per final guest capacity (engineer redesign if Phase 3 undersized)
- [ ] Water treatment: UV or chlorination system for well water (if not County system)
- [ ] Kitchen facilities: Full commercial kitchen → Food Service Permit (separate application)

**Full Health Clearance:** Multi-department inspection (Health + Fire + Building). Organized Camp Permit + Food Service Permit (if meals provided).

## Pre-Inspection Preparation Checklist (Before Contacting Health Dept)

- [ ] **Site plan** (8.5" × 11" or larger): Show parcel boundary, structures, septic location, water supply, dumpster location
- [ ] **Calculations:** Expected guest capacity (# of sites × avg guests/site)
- [ ] **Utility specs:**
  - Water source (well, County, hauled) + capacity
  - Wastewater method (septic model, composting toilet model, portable service)
  - Greywater plan (if separate system)
- [ ] **Emergency contact:** Name, phone, address of operator
- [ ] **Photos:** Current state of parcel (cleared, trees, existing structures)
- [ ] **Phase timeline:** What infrastructure ready by when?
