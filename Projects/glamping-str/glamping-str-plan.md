# Glamping STR Business Plan - Santa Cruz Mountains

## Executive Summary
Plan to establish a glamping (glamorous camping) short-term rental business on land in the Santa Cruz mountains, with father living on-site as caretaker/manager. Focus on unique, nature-immersive accommodations targeting couples and small groups seeking luxury outdoor experiences.

## Phase 1: Research & Planning (Weeks 1-4)

### 1.1 Legal & Regulatory Compliance
- Research Santa Cruz County short-term rental ordinances and permitting requirements
  - Check zoning for the specific parcel (likely requires conditional use permit)
  - Investigate glamping-specific regulations (may fall under recreational structures or temporary dwellings)
  - Review health and safety requirements for temporary lodging
  - Understand transient occupancy tax (TOT) obligations
- Consult with local land use attorney or planner familiar with Santa Cruz County regulations
- Determine if father can legally reside on-site as caretaker (may require accessory dwelling unit permissions)

### 1.2 Market Analysis
- Analyze existing glamping/STR competition in Santa Cruz mountains and surrounding areas
  - Airbnb/VRBO listings for similar properties
  - Established glamping resorts (e.g., Under Canvas, AutoCamp)
- Identify target demographics: couples, small groups, eco-tourists, wine country visitors
- Estimate seasonal demand patterns and pricing ranges
- Evaluate proximity to attractions: beaches, wineries, hiking trails, state parks

### 1.3 Business Model Definition
- Decide on glamping structure types (yurts, safari tents, tiny homes, A-frames, treehouses)
- Determine service level: fully serviced (linens, toiletries, breakfast) vs. self-catering
- Consider unique selling propositions: stargazing platforms, outdoor showers, fire pits, guided nature walks
- Establish pricing strategy based on market research and cost structure

## Phase 2: Site Preparation & Infrastructure (Weeks 5-12)

### 2.1 Land Assessment & Preparation
- Conduct topographic survey and soil testing
- Identify optimal structure placement considering views, privacy, drainage, and access
- Plan for erosion control and environmental protection measures
- Clear and grade sites as needed (minimize tree removal where possible)

### 2.2 Utilities & Infrastructure
- Water: Well drilling or connection to municipal supply if available; consider rainwater harvesting
- Power: Solar array with battery backup or grid connection; assess needs for each unit
- Septic System Options: Evaluate based on soil conditions, budget, and long-term plans:
    - **Haul-Away Holding Tank**: Lower upfront cost ($4,300-$11,300), requires regular pumping ($150-$400 per pump-out), suitable for testing market demand or poor soil conditions
    - **Conventional Septic with Leachfield**: Lower long-term cost, requires suitable soil (perc test 1-60 MPI), larger footprint
    - **Alternative Engineered Systems**: Mound systems, sand filters, ATUs, or drip distribution for challenging soils (higher cost, more maintenance)
    - **Composting Toilets + Graywater**: Water-saving option requiring separate systems
- Internet: Satellite or fixed wireless for reliable connectivity (essential for STR operations)
- Access: Improve driveway/access road for guest vehicles and emergency services
- Landscaping: Native, drought-tolerant planting; create outdoor living spaces

### 2.3 Structure Installation
- Obtain necessary permits for each glamping unit type
- Install foundations/platforms (raised decks, concrete pads, or gravel bases)
- Procure and install glamping structures (consider prefabricated options for faster deployment)
- Equip each unit with:
  - Quality bedding and linens
  - Furniture appropriate to climate
  - Lighting (solar-powered or wired)
  - Heating/cooling solutions (propane patio heaters, fans, or mini-splits)
  - Outdoor amenities (fire pit, seating, grill)

## Phase 3: Operations Setup (Weeks 9-14)

### 3.1 Management Systems
- Implement property management software (e.g., Hostfully, Guesty, or Airbnb's native tools)
- Set up dynamic pricing tool (e.g., Beyond Pricing, PriceLabs)
- Create cleaning and maintenance schedules
- Establish inventory management for linens, toiletries, supplies
- Develop guest communication templates (pre-arrival, check-in, during stay, post-stay)

### 3.2 Staffing & Training
- Define father's role as caretaker: responsibilities, compensation, living arrangements
- Determine if additional staff needed (cleaners, maintenance, guest services)
- Create operational manuals and training procedures
- Establish emergency procedures and local service contacts (plumbers, electricians, etc.)

### 3.3 Legal & Financial Setup
- Form appropriate business entity (LLC recommended for liability protection)
- Obtain necessary business licenses and permits
- Set up business banking and accounting systems
- Implement transient occupancy tax collection and remittance process
- Obtain appropriate insurance (liability, property, business interruption)

## Phase 4: Launch & Marketing (Weeks 13-16+)

### 4.1 Pre-Launch Preparations
- Professional photography of properties and surroundings
- Create compelling listings on Airbnb, VRBO, Hipcamp, and Glamping Hub
- Develop direct booking website (consider Squarespace, Wix, or WordPress)
- Establish social media presence (Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest)
- Set up review generation and management process

### 4.2 Soft Launch & Testing
- Host friends/family for trial stays to identify and fix issues
- Refine check-in/check-out procedures
- Test all systems (booking, payment, communication, utilities)
- Gather feedback and make necessary adjustments

### 4.3 Official Launch & Marketing Campaign
- Launch with special introductory rates to generate initial reviews
- Targeted advertising: Facebook/Instagram ads to couples interested in unique getaways
- Partnerships with local businesses (wineries, restaurants, tour operators)
- Content marketing: blog about Santa Cruz area attractions, glamping tips
- Consider listing with specialized glamping platforms

## Ongoing Operations & Optimization

### Monthly Tasks
- Review financial performance against projections
- Adjust pricing based on demand and competitor activity
- Maintain properties and perform preventive maintenance
- Refresh linens and supplies as needed
- Respond to guest reviews and implement improvement suggestions

### Quarterly Tasks
- Deep clean and inspect all units
- Update marketing materials and photos seasonally
- Review and update local partnerships
- Assess and implement guest feedback

### Annual Tasks
- Renew permits and licenses
- Major maintenance or upgrades
- Business planning for next year
- Tax preparation and filing

## Risk Mitigation

### Regulatory Risks
- Maintain ongoing compliance with changing STR regulations
- Consider forming relationship with other local STR operators for advocacy
- Have contingency plan if regulations become restrictive
- **Septic System Compliance**: Ensure all wastewater systems (haul-away, conventional, or alternative) meet Santa Cruz County Environmental Health requirements
- **Permitting**: Secure necessary permits for septic installation and operation; maintain documentation for inspections and approvals

### Operational Risks
- Develop robust maintenance schedule to prevent system failures
- Create weather contingency plans (especially for winter storms)
- Establish clear house rules and security measures
- Maintain adequate insurance coverage
- **Septic Maintenance**: Implement regular pumping schedule for haul-away systems or inspection schedule for conventional systems
- **Monitoring**: Install tank level alarms and conduct regular checks to prevent overflows or backups
- **Contingency Planning**: Have backup plans for septic system failures (e.g., alternative pumping services, temporary restrictions)

### Financial Risks
- Conservative financial projections with adequate cash reserves
- Diversify booking channels to reduce platform dependency
- Consider off-season promotions or packages to maintain occupancy
- **Septic Costs**: Budget for ongoing pumping expenses ($150-$400 per pump-out) if using haul-away system
- **Reserve Funds**: Set aside capital for potential septic system upgrades or transitions to conventional/alternative systems
- **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Evaluate long-term costs of haul-away vs. conventional septic based on occupancy projections

## Success Metrics
- Occupancy rate >60% after first year
- Average daily rate meeting or exceeding market average
- Guest satisfaction rating >4.8/5
- Positive cash flow within 18 months
- Father satisfied with caretaker role and compensation
