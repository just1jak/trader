## 2026‑05‑08 – Regulatory research completed
- Retrieved official Santa Cruz County Code definitions:
  - **Permanent Structure** – SCC Chapter 10.16, Section 10.16.030. URL: https://library.municode.com/ca/santa_cruz_county/codes/code_of_ordinances (Chapter 10.16).
  - **Commercial Trigger** – SCC Chapter 13.10 (Zoning Regulations). Campgrounds with **> 5 campsites** require a Conditional Use Permit. URL: https://www.codepublishing.com/CA/SantaCruzCounty/html/SantaCruzCounty13/SantaCruzCounty1310.html.
- Confirmed parcel lies within the **California Coastal Zone**; CEQA/Coastal Commission review applies. Zoning Information & Clearances portal: https://cdi.santacruzcountyca.gov/UPC/DiscretionalPermitsZoning/ZoningInformationClearances.aspx.
- Identified permit workflow for a campground on Special‑Use land:
  1. **Conditional Use Permit (CUP)** – required for any campground. Application portal: https://www.santacruzcountyca.gov/Planning/ConditionalUsePermit (exact URL to be confirmed on County site).
  2. **Organized Camp Permit** – required if the site operates > 5 days per season (SCC Chapter 7.44). Application portal: https://www.santacruzcountyca.gov/EnvironmentalHealth/OrganizedCamp (to be verified).
  3. **Short‑Term Rental (STR) registration** – required for nightly rentals. Registration portal: https://www.santacruzcountyca.gov/Planning/ShortTermRental.
  4. **Building / Zoning permits** – for any permanent or semi‑permanent structures (platforms, toilets, utilities). Submit via the County Building Services portal: https://www.santacruzcountyca.gov/Building.
- Mapped the **permit timeline**:
  - STR registration: 2‑4 weeks.
  - CUP: 3‑6 months (public notice, hearing, CEQA/Coastal review).
  - Organized Camp Permit: concurrent with CUP if > 5 days usage.

## 2026-05-09 – SU Zoning Analysis Completed
- Researched SCC Chapter 13.20 (Special Use District) restrictions for APN 086-631-14.
- **Key Finding:** SU zoning is more restrictive than RA zoning for glamping operations. ALL commercial uses require CUP.
- **Allowed Uses on SU (no permit):** Single-family residence, agriculture/forestry only.
- **Conditional Uses on SU (require CUP):** Campgrounds, glamping, guest ranches, STRs.
- **Permit Hierarchy:** Land Use Consistency → CUP Application → Hearing Examiner → Organized Camp Permit (if >5 sites) → Building/Fire/Health permits.
- **Timeline Impact:** CUP adds 3–6 months and $2–5K in application + hearing costs.
- **Approval Dependencies:** Hearing Examiner must find public benefit + land-use compatibility with SU district character (Chap 13.28 standards).
- Full analysis documented in REGULATORY_LOG.md. Next: Investigate RA zoning for APN 086-521-02 (likely easier permit path).
